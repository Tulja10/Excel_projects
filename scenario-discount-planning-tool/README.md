## Discount Approval Scenario Planning Tool - using Excel

## Overview

This Excel-based scenario planning tool helps a sales manager evaluate whether offering a discount to a customer is financially viable.

The model simulates different sales outcomes and calculates the resulting **revenue, costs, and gross margin impact** before approving a deal.
The business case is based on a fictional negotiation with a retail client (e.g., Croma) for keyboard product sets.

## Business Problem

During sales negotiations, discounts are often offered to close deals.
However, approving discounts without analyzing their impact can reduce profitability.

This tool helps answer key questions:

* How will the discount affect revenue and margins?
* Will the deal still meet the company’s margin targets?
* What happens if sales volume is lower than expected?

  

## Scenario Analysis

The model evaluates three possible outcomes:

* **Best Case** – High sales volume
* **Realistic Case** – Expected sales volume
* **Worst Case** – Low sales volume

Each scenario automatically calculates the financial impact based on the selected inputs.



## Key Inputs

Users can adjust several parameters to simulate different negotiation outcomes:

* Customer
* Product
* Discount %
* COGS % (Cost of Goods Sold)
* Average Net Invoice Price
* Gross Margin Target



## Key Metrics

The tool calculates and compares:

* Net Sales
* Post-Discount Revenue
* Cost of Goods Sold
* Gross Margin
* Margin vs Target

Visual indicators highlight whether the margin target is achieved.



## Excel Skills Demonstrated

* Scenario modelling
* What-If analysis
* Financial calculations
* Conditional formatting
* Decision-support reporting



## Dataset

The dataset used in this project is **randomly generated for demonstration purposes**.
No real company or customer data is used.



## Purpose

This project demonstrates how Excel can be used as a **decision-support tool for pricing and sales negotiations**, helping managers evaluate the financial impact of discounts before approving deals.
